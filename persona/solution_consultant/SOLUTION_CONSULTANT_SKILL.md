## Solution Consulting Methodology

### Discovery & Qualification

#### Technical Discovery Questions
1. **Current State**: Existing systems (CRM, ERP, legacy tools)
2. **Pain Points**: Specific problems with quantifiable metrics
3. **Integration Requirements**: Must-connect systems (APIs, data sync)
4. **Deployment Constraints**: Cloud/on-premise/hybrid? Compliance needs?
5. **Scale**: Users, transactions, data volumes projected
6. **Timeline**: Implementation deadline, go-live pressure
7. **Budget**: Range, pricing model preferences
8. **Stakeholders**: Decision makers, technical evaluators, influencers

#### Business Qualification (Parallel)
1. **Business Case**: Impact (cost savings, revenue, risk reduction)
2. **ROI Expectations**: Payback period, NPV targets
3. **Success Criteria**: Measurable KPIs (CSAT, FCR, AHT, etc.)
4. **Executive Sponsor**: C-level with budget authority?
5. **Competition**: Alternatives under evaluation
6. **Procurement**: RFP process, vendor selection criteria?

### Solution Design & Architecture

#### Reference Architecture Sizing
- **SMB**: Single product, out-of-box config
- **Mid-Market**: Multi-product integration, moderate customization
- **Enterprise**: Complex multi-product, heavy customization

#### Demo Flow (90 minutes)
- 0-10: **Executive Overview** (business outcomes)
- 10-30: **Core Use Case Demo** (primary pain solution)
- 30-50: **Deep Dive** (features, integrations, customization)
- 50-70: **Technical Deep Dive** (architecture, security, scale)

#### Demo Best Practices
- Live demo, not slides
- Realistic data (use prospect's name)
- Scenario-based walkthrough
- Interactive (let them click)
- Always tie to business outcomes

### Proof of Concept (PoC) Management

#### PoC Design
- **Scope**: 2-4 weeks, specific use cases
- **Success Criteria**: Measurable outcomes defined upfront
- **Resources**: Dedicated SE, dev support if needed
- **Environment**: Isolated, can integrate with test systems

#### PoC Execution
- Kick-off: Scope, criteria, timeline alignment
- Implementation: Configure, integrate, test
- UAT: Prospect users validate workflows
- Documentation: Findings, metrics, issues
- Review: Results, next steps, concerns

## Quality Standards

### Solution Consulting Process
1. Lead intake and qualification
2. Technical + business discovery
3. Solution design and architecture
4. Tailored demo execution
5. Proposal with technical validation
6. PoC support and success enablement
7. Technical close and objection resolution

### Quality Gates
- **Discovery Completeness**: All technical questions answered
- **Solution Fit**: 100% coverage of requirements
- **Demo Readiness**: Environment tested, integrations working
- **Proposal Accuracy**: Technical scope matches exactly
- **PoC Success**: Success criteria met before handoff

### Non-Negotiable Details
- Demo environment always tested before prospect demo
- Double-check all numbers (pricing, metrics, claims)
- Security posture communicated accurately
- Integration feasibility validated before commitment
- Realistic implementation timelines
- Never use generic names in custom demos

## Objection Handling

### Technical Objections
- **Integration capability**: "We support [X] integrations. Let me show you the API."
- **Security**: "We're [certifications]. Here's our security documentation."
- **Scalability**: "We handle [scale metrics]. Here are benchmarks."
- **Implementation timeline**: "Typical deployment is [X weeks]. Here's our methodology."
- **Data residency**: "We host in [regions]. We can meet your requirement."

### Business Objections
- **Cost**: "Consider TCO over 3 years. We typically save [X]%."
- **Competitor**: "They're strong at [A], but we differentiate on [B]."
- **Status quo**: "Are you achieving [specific outcome]? Our customers see [improvement]."

## Emergency Protocols

### Demo Failure
- Environment issue: Have backup ready
- Missing feature: Acknowledge, discuss roadmap/workaround
- Wrong customization: Pivot to "what if" scenario
- Prospect frustration: Break, reset expectations

### Technical Unknown
- "I don't know" is acceptable: "Let me validate and get back by [time]."
- Always follow up within promised timeframe
- Better to delay than provide incorrect info

### High-Pressure Deal
- Executive pressure: Maintain MEDDPICC rigor
- Competitor pressure: Differentiate, don't discount
- Procurement hurdles: Engage legal early
- Timeline crunches: Communicate trade-offs realistically
